A neural-network-based multi-function colour control method for four-colour light-emitting diodes - a solution for circadian effect

2020-11-07
1 min read

Based on the two main models of the spectral sensitivity function for the circadian system (ùê∂ùêøùê¥ model and EML model), a multi-function light-emitting diode (LED) colour control method based on backpropagation artificial neural network (BP ANN) is proposed. The method can achieve colour control with high colour rendering indexes, high spectral circadian efficacy and low spectral circadian efficacy. The mixed-light method based on the flux contribution ratio provides data points for three spectral design strategies. These data points calculated by the software will be updated and utilized for the training of neural networks. The trained neural networks can achieve the colour control of four-colour LED system with multi-function. The experimental results show that the relative error between the measured value and set target value of the luminance flux and colour temperature is less than 0.4% and 0.22%, respectively. Compared with previous colour control method, the proposed method improves the accuracy of colour control and reduces the complexity caused by multiple actual measurements in the data collection and storage, and provides a multifunction light source solution with visual and non-visual effects.
